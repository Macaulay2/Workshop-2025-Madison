Macaulay2 Workshop: Madison 2025

June 30 - July 4, 2025

Organizers: Thomas Brazelton & Mahrud Sayrafi
& Thomas Yahl

    

Information

There will be a one-week Macaulay2 workshop held at University of Wisconsin - Madison, which will take place the week before SIAM-AG 2025. Persons of all levels of Macaulay2 experience and knowledge are encouraged to apply. We especially encourage the participation of those from underrepresented groups and ethnicities. This workshop will aim to serve several purposes:

  • To expand the capabilities and functionality of Macaulay2 and its accompanying packages.
  • To bring together members of diverse mathematical communities and begin new collaborations.
  • To provide students with hands-on learning and coding experiences and to prepare them to attend the subsequent SIAM-AG 2025 meeting.


Room Notices:

  • All talks will be located in Van Vleck B130.

  • Projects "InvariantRing 3.0", "Weighted and Equivariant Ehrhart Theory", "Homological algebra over the exterior algebra", and "C_p-Mackey Functors" will work in Sterling 2425.

  • Projects "Expanding interval arithmetic in Macaulay2", "Elimination Templates", and "Traces and norms of symmetric bilinear forms" will work in Sterling 2301.

Funding Information

This event is made possible thanks to funding from the National Science Foundation (DMS-2508868). We are also grateful to the Department of Mathematics at the University of Wisconsin - Madison.

We are expecting to be able to assist a limited number of graduate students and postdocs traveling to Madison. More specifically, we hope to provide lodging and up to $400 travel expenses for some participants. If you would like to apply for financial assistance, please indicate this need on the registration form below.
